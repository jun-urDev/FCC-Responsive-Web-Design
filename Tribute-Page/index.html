Career Timeline

Amateur Career

  • 2007 - Vasyl Lomachenko began his amateur career at the 2007 World Championships in Chicago.
  • 2008 - He won the gold medal at 2008 Olympic Games in Beijing, China and received the 'Val Barker Trophy' as the tournament's outstanding boxer. He also won every match at the 2008 European Championships.
  • 2009 - He won the featherweight division title at the World Championships.
  • 2011 - At the 2011 World Championships, he participated in the lightweight division and went on to earn another gold medal.
  • 2012 - He bagged his second consecutive Gold Medal at 2012 London Olympics.
  • 2013 - He competed in the lightweight division of the World Series of Boxing (WSB) briefly in the early 2013. He ended his amateur career at 396 wins and 1 loss.

Professional Career

  • 2013 - Vasyl Lomachenko signed a contract with Top Rank and made his professional debut.
  • 2014 - He attempted but failed to make world record by challenging veteran boxer Orlando Salido for the World Boxing Organization's featherweight title.
  • 2014 - Lomachenko defeated Gary Rusell Jr. to become the new featherweight champion.
  • 2016 - He moved to the super-featherweight division and defeated Román 'Rocky' Martínez and became the fastest boxer to win a world title in two weight divisions.
  • 2016 - He made his opponents Nicholas Walters, Jason Sosa, Miguel Marriaga, and fellow 2-time Olympic Gold Medalist Guillermo Rigondeaux quit during their bouts which granted him the name 'Nomaschenko'.
  • 2018 - Lomachenko moved up to the lightweight division and claimed the WBA (Super) and 'The Ring' lightweight titles by defeating Jorge Linares becoming the fastest 3-weight world champion at the time.
  • 2020 - He was regarded the 'undisputed' lightweight world champion until he suffered his 2nd career defeat to Teofimo Lopez.
